Use Microsoft Teams and Confluence together

Atlassian Government environment

You can soon
create, edit, and share Confluence pages and live docs without leaving Microsoft Teams.
Need to quickly find a Confluence page or doc? Add it to a tab.
You can even let other teammates know about it too.
Collaborating in Confluence without leaving Microsoft Teams
It’s a time-saving way to collaborate with your teammates. And helps avoid the dreaded “too many open browsers” overload.
Create and edit pages and live docs
. Minimizes context-switching.
Add a page or doc in a tab
. An easy way to keep track without going back and forth between browsers.
Get notified about edits and new content
. Stay in the know.
People can preview content you share
. Take a quick look or read all the info right away.
Search within Teams for previously shared pages and live docs
. Information is never lost.
Capture and share meeting notes as a group
. Make decisions and agree on action items together.
Get started
To add the Confluence Cloud for Microsoft Teams app:
Open your Microsoft Teams app.
Select
Apps
in the bottom right corner.
Search
Confluence Cloud.
Select
Open
and follow the prompts to connect it.
To share Confluence pages and live docs in Teams channels:
Go to the chat window.
Select
…
Type in Confluence Cloud to add the app.
You need to join Confluence to collaborate on content within Microsoft Teams. When you connect the app, you can request access to Confluence from your Confluence admin.
Create a new Confluence page
Select
Teams
tab in the left panel.
Select one of your channels.
Click
+
in the top right.
Click
Confluence Cloud for Microsoft Teams app
.
Select
Create page
Select the
Confluence site
, then a
Parent page
.
Add your page title and start writing.
You can choose to save as a draft, share, and publish - it’s the same way you’d normally create a page, you’re just doing it from within Microsoft Teams.
It’s not currently possible to create a new live doc from Teams.
Need to update page permissions? Set them directly in Confluence.
Add an existing Confluence page or live doc
If you have multiple Confluence sites, you'll need to choose which site to search for content from.
Select
Teams
tab in the left panel.
Select one of your channels.
Click
+
in the top right.
Click
Confluence Cloud for Microsoft Teams app
.
Select
Attach page
or
doc.
Search by title or choose a recently viewed page.
You can select
Post to the channel about this tab
.
Select
Save
.
Share a Confluence page or live doc in a channel
Start a new conversation with someone or your team.
Click the Confluence Cloud icon at the bottom.
Select which page or doc you want to share.
Type message and send.
Use Confluence during a Teams meeting
Click
Calendar
in the left menu.
Click
New Meeting.
Create a
meeting title
and add
required attendees
(You'll need to add someone to the invitation first).
Click
Send
and you'll see the meeting on your Calendar.
Open the meeting and click
+
in the top right.
Select
Confluence Cloud
to add notes to your meeting.
Click
Add.
Click
Save.
You'll see a button for
Start Notes
to show you've successfully added the Confluence app to your meeting.
Take notes
You can start taking notes before, during, or after the meeting.
Select
Start Notes.
Select
Join
will allow you to enter a meeting.
Select the
Confluence logo
at the top right - that will open a side panel for you to take notes during your meeting.
Select
Publish
then choose which Space to publish the notes to.
After your meeting ends, the notes you took will also be sent to your meeting conversation.
Uninstall the app
You’ll see the
Confluence logo
in the left panel.
Right-click the icon.
Select
Uninstall.
Confirm
Uninstall.
